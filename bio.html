Brett Armstrong is a Doctoral student at the University of Colorado – Boulder, where he is employed as a Teaching Assistant to Paul Erhard, his current teacher and mentor. Brett received his Masters of Music for the University of Colorado in 2016. Before his move to Colorado, Brett received his Bachelor of Music from Western Michigan University (Magna Cum Laude) while studying under Tom Knific. Brett won the WMU annual soloist competition and appeared as a soloist with the University Symphony Orchestra. He has also appeared as a soloist in public concerts and competitions, including the International Sperger Competition.

In addition to solo work, Brett plays a wide variety of Chamber music from medieval to modern. Brett was a member of the contemporary new music group Birds on a Wire during his time at Western Michigan University, and recorded Paul Lansky’s “Contemplating Weather” with them. Brett has also had a love of early music for most of his career, and has played with various early music groups on the Bass and the Viol da Gamba. Brett is currently playing a baroque style Bass as a fellowship artist of the Boulder Bach Festival.

As an orchestral musician, Brett is currently principal of both the Cheyenne Symphony Orchestra and the Greeley Philharmonic Orchestra, and is a section player in Fort Collins Symphony. Brett also plays regularly with Colorado Bach Ensemble, Pro Musica Colorado, and the Colorado Symphony Orchestra. In Michigan, Brett was an Artist-Scholar of the Kalamazoo Symphony Orchestra and was a substitute in several surrounding orchestras.
